What I Do

I'm a developer and IT specialist. I build software — full-stack web and desktop applications — and I support and secure the systems it runs on. That combination is the point: most technical support stops at keeping things running, and I also build the tools that make them run better.

Right now I operate enterprise access control systems at Hunter College's Visitor Center, where I was independently selected to design and build the department's first digital training platform — a full-stack application I rebuilt as a secure desktop build and presented to department leadership. I hold a B.S. in Computer Science from Hunter College and CompTIA A+, with Security+ in progress.

I'm looking to join a team in an IT, systems, or analyst role — at a law firm, a financial institution, or a tech company — where building internal tools and automating the tedious work is expected, not treated as a bonus.

I'm good with people, and it isn't luck. I grew up working customer-facing on New York's sidewalks from a young age — about as demanding a service environment as exists. It taught me to read people quickly, stay composed under pressure, and make technical things make sense to anyone.
