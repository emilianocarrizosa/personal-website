About

Background & Trajectory

I began my career at age four, working alongside family as a street vendor in New York City. From elementary school through the end of high school, the sidewalks of this city were my first professional environment — where I developed customer service instincts, composure under pressure, and the ability to communicate effectively with every kind of person, long before I had a technical credential to my name.

Today I operate enterprise access control systems at Hunter College's Visitor Center — one of New York City's premier public universities — providing direct support to over 200 visitors daily, including VIP guests and the President's Office. I hold CompTIA A+ and am actively pursuing Security+, MS-900, and ITIL 4, expected April 2026.

My long-term objective is operational infrastructure security in its totality: managing and securing the full stack of an organization's operational systems — physical access control, network security, endpoint hardening, and enterprise IT governance at a senior level. I'm building toward that from the ground up, with intention.

I'm guided by the conviction that work is a privilege, not a given — and that anything worth doing is worth doing at the highest level possible. That standard is non-negotiable regardless of the task at hand.
